Traffic Rules

As the traffic is increasing day by day in the country, the government is making new roads for a better environment and to avoid such hustle and bustle on the roads. Speeding is defined as the act or an instance of driving, especially a motor vehicle, faster than is allowed by law..

Traffic rules of the roads are both the laws and the informal rules that may have been developed over time to facilitate the orderly and timely flow of traffic.

It is necessary to have rules everywhere in order to make the functioning smooth and efficient.

Traffic signs

  • Mandatory traffic signs in India are made to ensure the traffic moves freely. With the knowledge of these signs, the drivers come to know about the prohibitions and the rules and regulations which they need to keep in mind while driving on the road.
  • If any of the traffic rules are violated, it is considered to be an offence. Some of the mandatory traffic signs are “Stop”, “No Entry”, “One Way”, “Right Turn Prohibited”, “Speed Limit” etc.
  • These are the Indian traffic signs with their meanings which inform the driver about distance, directions, alternative routes, destinations and important places like hospitals, schools etc.

Signals

For allowing the people on the opposite side to cross safely and also permit the automobile on the backside to go beyond, we need to keep the vehicle to the left.

Traffic lights are signaling devices located at road intersections and pedestrian crossings, they are now part of our everyday life.

Lower the speed of the vehicle at the turnings, where there are a lot of blockages, at the endings of the road, at zebra crossings, on the occasion of overtaking and such other instances.
